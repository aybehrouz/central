\newcommand{\engFunc}[1][P]{\measure{F}{}{Q,\tilde{#1}}}


Suppose we want to approximate the central distribution of a hierarchy of distributions \set{h}.
To do so, we need a measure that quantifies the quality of an approximation.
We argue that a natural choice is the central divergence introduced in Definition~\ref{def:central-divergence}.

At first glance, it appears that computing this quantity requires knowledge of the central distribution of \set{h}.
However, it turns out that the central divergence admits an upper bound that can be estimated without explicitly
knowing the central distribution.

In variational settings, it is often more convenient to work with unnormalized measures rather than normalized
distributions.
Therefore, we state the next theorem in terms of unnormalized measures.

\begin{theorem}\label{th:central-approx}
    Suppose that the central distribution \cDist from Theorem~\ref{th:centrality} exists,
    Let $\tilde{P}$ be a nonnegative linear combination of elements of \setQ,
    and let $P$ be the corresponding normalized distribution.
    Then
    \[
        \myDiv[][Q] \leq \sup_{\inSet{Q}} \engFunc - \inf_{\inSet{Q}} \engFunc,
    \]
    where
    \[
        \engFunc = \entropy{Q}{X} + \Ex{Q}{\ln \tilde{P}(X)}.
    \]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
    By Theorem~\ref{th:centrality}, since $C$ is the unique central distribution of \setQ,
    from Equation~\ref{eq:cd-relation-to-lambda}, we have
    \[
        \myDiv[][Q] \leq \supD - \lambda.
    \]
    On the other hand, since $P$ is a convex combination of elements of \setQ,
    Lemma~\ref{lm:imperfection} implies that
    \[
        \inf_{\inSet{Q}} \kl \leq \lambda.
    \]
    Combining these two inequalities yields
    \begin{equation} \label{eq:cdiv-upper-bound}
        \myDiv[][Q] \leq \supD - \inf_{\inSet{Q}} \kl.
    \end{equation}

    Now let $\tilde{P}(X) = Z P(X)$, where $Z > 0$ is a normalization constant.
    Then, for any $Q$,
    \[
        \kl = \ln Z - \engFunc.
    \]
    Since $\ln Z$ does not depend on $Q$, the supremum and infimum translate directly into the corresponding
    expressions in terms of $\measure{F}{}{}$, and the result follows from Equation~\ref{eq:cdiv-upper-bound}.
\end{proof}

The theorem shows that we seek a $\tilde{P}$ such that $\engFunc$ is approximately constant over \inSet{Q}.
This constant can be arbitrary, but it is convenient if it is zero, in which case
\[
    \Ex{Q}{\ln \tilde{P}(X)} \approx - \entropy{Q}{X}.
\]

Note that Theorem~\ref{th:central-approx} requires $\tilde{P}$ to be a nonnegative linear
combination of elements of \setQ.
The bound is valid only if such a $\tilde{P}$ exists for which the equality condition is satisfied exactly.
